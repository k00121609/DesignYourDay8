- TIC-TAC-TOE Tooltips -

This game is otherwise know as 'X & O's.' It is usually played as a 'paper and pencil' game for two players. The players take turns marking spaces in a '3 x 3 grid.' Use the mouse to click on the space you wish to mark. The player who succeeds in placing 3 of their marks, in a horizontal, vertical or diagonal row wins the game.

- N-Blox Tooltips -

This is a single player game for 'you' the player. Use the 'arrow keys' to orientate and place the blocks. Press the 'space' key for fast-drop. The level increases every 10 lines. There are options of: play, high scores, quit. When you click 'play' there are options to play at different levels. Levels are from 1-10, where 10 is the highest level.

- PACMAN Tooltips -

In this game 'you' the 'player' controls Pac-Man through a maze, eating pac-dots. When all the pac-dots are eaten, the game goes to the next level. There are 4 enemies that roam the maze, trying to catch your Pac-Man, which you must avoid. If you eat a power-pill, you can eat the ghosts! Eating a fruit when it appears gives you a bonue score. The controls are the: arrow keys, 'p' for pause/unpause, 'q' for quit game, 'm' for mute/unmute.

- Simon Says Tooltips -

This game has 4 coloured buttons, each producing a particular sound when it is pressed or activated by the device. Follow the pattern of the lights and sounds for as long as you can. A round in the game consists of the device lighting up one or more buttons in a random order. You the player must reproduce that order by pressing the buttons. If you succeed the series becomes progressively longer and more complex. If you miss a pattern the game is over.
