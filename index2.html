IT & our Health System

Heath Information Technology (HIT) is information technology applied to health and healthcare. Here, technology represents computers and communications attributes that can be networked to build systems for moving health information.

Closely related is Health Informatics, refers to the science of information, the practice of information processing and the engineering of information systems. It has the same objectives of HIT and marks the convergence of: Healthcare, IT and Business.

HIT functions are in support of health information management including, efficiency, accuracy and healthcare applications of documentation, education and self-care, and in meeting this demand in society.

What is my BMI about?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a way of presenting a persons weight status, based on a calculation of their weight and height.

It is an established method used in Healthcare to determine whether a person is underweight, overweight or within a healthy weight range. This applies to adult women and men and can be an indicator of your health and well-being.

In using he BMI calculator you can select "metric" or "imperial" unit measurement.


Mindfulness and Meditation

Mindfulness is the overall focusing concept while meditation is more technique specific e.g. Yoga, while both are part of our popular culture today.

Mindfulness is where we are deliberately paying attention to our experience as it unfolds. It is not always about focusing on the positive. In being mindful, we are interested in all our experiences -good-bad-neutral.

Mindfulness also involves being aware of our 'narratives,' the stories we tell our-selves that can be streaming live in the background. Our interpretation of these can simply be based on how we are feeling at a time.

It has been found that the curiosity in exploring a feeling, may help us in being less overwhelmed by it. As one of the most reliable things in life is change, "Calm is a Superpower."


Monthly Featured Activity-River Walk

Mindfulness and activity have long been associated with good health. Physical activity is a feature of mindfulness. In adults it is associated with a lower risk of obesity, cardiovascular disease, high blood pressure, diabetes, cancer and premature death.

Such activity may indirectly influence health behaviours such as overeating, smoking, substance abuse, stress management, risk taking and others. It is also correlated with reduction in signs of anxiety and depression.

Only a few lifestyle choices have as large an impact on a persons health as physical activity.

People who are physically active for about 7 hours a week have a 40% lower risk of dying early than those who are active for less than 30 minutes a week.
